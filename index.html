Sound System Mark 1

In tutorial, after we begun construction, we soon realized how hard this may be. We can't have any light get passed the sound system once it is contained in the projector shell. However, the sound system needs to have holes to allow the sound to amplify and escape.
Because we can't have holes in the front of the sound system, we will need to use the back of the projector box as the shell of the sound amplifier itself. We make a stand to transmit the sound back into the projector box and let the projector box act as an amplifier.

The Second Meeting

The group discussed ideas on how to proceed with the building. We design the conceptual ideas that we brainstormed during the first meeting and solidify abstract ideas. The main theme of this meeting was to work on the projector 'part' of this contraption. We divided up the space of the amazon box into 2 halves, one for the projector and the other for a 3-D hologram projector...thing. With actual materials, contact glue, a hot glue gun, scissors, Exact-o knife and many boxes, we dedicated hours of this meeting to cutting, scissoring, gluing, testing out the contraption. We cut many corners since this was an initial stage to making our project. Image 5.0 is the initial conceptualization when we began this meeting. Image 6.0 is the aftermath of the mass murder of cardboard boxes.
